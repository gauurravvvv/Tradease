The best traders I know don't trade emotionally. They follow rules. Every single time.

I realized I couldn't do that -- but a machine could.

So I stopped trading. And built something that trades the way I wish I could.

Tradease -- an AI-powered autonomous trading system for Indian F&O markets. You start it. You walk away.

3 AI agents run in the background:

The Strategist -- scans 180+ F&O stocks across 9 factors, confirms signals on multiple timeframes, enters the trade. No hesitation.

The Guardian -- monitors every position every 2 minutes. SL hit? Exit. Target hit? Book profits. Nifty crashes? Exit everything. 3:15 PM? Close all. Zero emotion.

The Sentinel -- reads news in real-time. Bearish on your holding? Signals the Guardian. Bullish on a candidate? Boosts entry signal.

They talk through a signal database. No human in the loop.

What's packed inside:

-> Adaptive trailing stops (reads RSI + MACD momentum)
-> Multi-timeframe confluence (5m, 15m, 1h, daily)
-> Real-time dashboard -- live charts, equity curve, risk panel, agent monitor
-> Backtesting -- 3 strategies, Sharpe ratio, drawdown, profit factor
-> Auto trade journal with tags and ratings
-> Telegram alerts for every entry, exit, stop-loss, target hit
-> Hard risk rules -- max 3 positions, 20% per trade, ATR stops. Non-negotiable.
-> 90%+ decisions are rule-based. AI called only for edge cases -- 2-3x/day, cheapest model.

Tech: Node.js, Claude AI, Yahoo Finance, SQLite. ~6,000 lines. One command: tradease daemon

Current status: Testing phase -- paper trading with Rs 2,00,000 virtual capital. Monitoring how agents behave, what they pick, where the logic breaks.

What's next: Integrate with a real demat account -- start small, limited capital, let it trade live. The risk management is already built for it. Just needs enough testing cycles.
